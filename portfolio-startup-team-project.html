Mobile App Product Design in a Startup

A story of product ownership, teamwork, and vision.

Project description:

  • Design, and manage the development of, a mobile app product for a new startup venture (and co-found the company).
  • This product will be the core offering of the company.
  • iOS and Android mobile apps will be developed in parallel, and the product will contain several innovative feature sets that are protected by an NDA.
  • To honor that NDA, I have chosen to focus on my work responsibilities themselves, and the role I've played in the progress of the startup so far.

My roles:

  • Product Manager & Product Owner
  • Product Designer & UX Designer
  • Co-Founder

Time alotted:

  • Ongoing
  • Ideation began in late 2016

Team size:

  • ~ 15 people internally (including 3 co-founders, with the whole team working on equity)
  • our offshore development team

My responsibilities:

  1. Assessment of initial idea product-market fit
  2. Recommend and assist in set up of internal operational software
  3. Competitor analysis
  4. Expansion of product core features
  5. Definition of features built into the minimum viable product (MVP)
  6. Develop product versioning structure/nomenclature
  7. Work with team to confirm brand style is in line with product roadmap and company horizons
  8. Maintain product roadmap and list of upcoming features, maintaining alignment with business horizons
  9. Low fidelity wireframes of MVP
  10. Periodically research industry standards and new design concepts, with the goal of choosing the most usable design per feature
  11. Collaboration with team to develop high fidelity click-through design with user flow notes incorporated into it [estimated # o slides]
  12. User Research within the internal team
  13. Gather external and less-biased feedback, per feature idea, while keeping the product vision under NDA
  14. Create an internal workflow that gathers input from team on items like potential edge cases, monetization ideas, future feature ideas, design ideas
  15. Work with marketing to confirm that all in-app messaging is in alignment with external messaging
  16. Provide brand materials for social media accounts
  17. Document our Design Rules and share them with the company - these work in sync with the core mission and vision of the company
  18. Maintain list of factors that will contribute to future feature decisions, and how the will be incorporated into a living product roadmap
  19. Collaborate with strategy, finance, and marketing to design a monetization strategy
  20. Participate in fundraising pitch sessions via giving product demos
  21. Project management of initial product development with offshore team
  22. Regular testing of product versions
  23. Periodic gathering and sorting of team input

The product:

More than 90 high fidelity screens designed so far, hooked up in Adobe XD, with supplementary comments next to most of them.

The following is a screenshot of the product design, with all screens covered up and all comments blurred in order to honor the NDA.

Product documentation created:

  • Functional Specifciation
  • Product Roadmap
  • Priority List
  • Design Rules (for the app and for the brand)
  • Internal communication tool with marketing: spreadsheet of marketing-approved in-app copy
  • Future feature ideas submission workflow for the team internally
  • Archive of old product designs, for record-keeping purposes
  • Task management and sprint feedback workflow, for work with the internal design team, the offshore development team, and the internal app accpetance testing team

Lessons learned so far:

  • There are almost always more edge cases to design.
  • Feedback is almost always useful, especially neutrual feedback
  • Be in the best possible communication with other departments
  • Track tasks - it helps the team pick up pace to see how many there actually are
  • Design directly in Adobe XD (rather than in Photoshop) to build prototypes - it has gotten much more robust lately and is easier to edit along the way
  • Offshore teams can get the job done, but it is best to have a systms architech onshore who is up to date with modern frameworks
  • Create suscinct, very very detailed documentation, but don't let the early lack of it keep things from moving forward
  • Try to laugh more

Next steps:

  • Continue to build out the minimum viable product with the offshore team, while gathering ongoing product feedback and assisting other team members with implementing our launch strategy.
